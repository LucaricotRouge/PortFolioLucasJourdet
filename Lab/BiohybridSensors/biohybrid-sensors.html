Biohybrid Sensors

Project Overview

Based on the work of Nicolae et al."Biohybrid Devices" I replicate the "grow arround method" and "layering" to embed sensing capabilities in bacterial cellullose. In the course of this project I built a total of 3 different sensors, two of which were resistive (temperature and stretch sensors) and one capacitive (mutual capacitance matrix). Using the Arduino and other electronic components, I was able to design functional bio-hybrid sensors.

Research Focus

The project explores the intersection of biotechnology and material science to create innovative sensing solutions.

Key Achievements

  • Novel sensor design architecture
  • Biocompatible material selection
  • Real-time data processing
  • Clinical applications potential

Impact & Applications

This research opens new possibilities in medical diagnostics and environmental monitoring.
